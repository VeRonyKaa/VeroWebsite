Annotating Underwater Events of the Limfjord Through Crowdsourced Citizen Science

This project aims to engage people in mostly uninteresting topics that they do not know much about. It utilizes gamification methods to design a citizen science application that presents underwater events in an engaging way. The footage from the cameras runs through a motion detection algorithm that splits the video up into smaller video clips where an event is present. These clips are presented to the public through the application. The purpose of the application is to annotate these events, depending on what kind of marine life is present to create a database. The design of the application was twofold. First, a mental model elicitation was conducted in order to explore the users understanding and opinions of the app. Second, a test of the lo-fi prototype was carried out using the methods of task completion, think aloud and semi-structured interviews. After several alterations to the design a final design was drawn and implemented. In order to test the application, interviews and questionnaires were conducted. The interviews were analysed though coding and showed satisfying results in terms of user engagement and uncovered further room for research and improvement.

Designing Calorie Counters in HIT apps for Women

In this project the goal was to design a product for regularly exercising women between the ages of 20 and 26. After conducting a user-study on the target group, a calorie intake deficit was discovered amongst them. Therefore, a solution was designed in collaboration with health and fitness trainers to motivate women to eat a healthier number of calories. To accommodate these needs, we designed and implemented an app that provides a representation of the user’s daily caloric intake and calories they have burned. A prototype was implemented and tested on the target group. The evaluation was done by giving the users specific tasks to complete and asking them to answer two questionnaires. The quantitative and qualitative results were analysed and used to improve the app further.

Creating a digital painting system aimed to facilitate creative growth in children

This project explores the problem of decreased creativity levels within young children. We attempted to solve this problem by developing a prototype that uses modern technology to engage children in an artistic activity via finger painting. The prototype uses image processing to detect custom designed gestures and allows the user to paint with their fingers. The evaluation was focused on the technological criteria for the product, to ensure its proper functionality. Several ways on how the prototype could be improved were investigated and a user-focused evaluation was planned.

Comparing Input Methods When Typing on a Virtual Keyboard in VR.

This project explores and compares two different ray casting input methods when typing on a virtual keyboard in Virtual reality. These two methods are hand and head-hand directed and they both use ray casting to display a cursor on the keyboard plane. After finding the most effective setup of the keyboard for both methods in the experimental design phase, an evaluation of the final product was conducted with 30 participants. The collected data was analysed and an insignificant difference in the Total Error Rate was found. However, additional variables were documented, and their analysis aided in finding the overall better method.
